1. Overview

Buying a home is one of the biggest decisions you'll make. It can seem complicated, but breaking the journey down into clear steps makes it much easier. This guide walks you through each stage of the process.

2. The Step-by-Step Process

🧮
Step 1: Check Your Finances & Budget

Review your income, expenses, debts and savings. Understand how much you can afford including deposit and buying costs.

Read more →
🏦
Step 2: Get a Mortgage Agreement in Principle

A mortgage agreement in principle (AIP) shows how much you can borrow and strengthens your position when making an offer.

Read more →
🔍
Step 3: Find a Property

Search for properties online, go for viewings and shortlist the homes that best meet your needs.

Read more →
💰
Step 4: Make an Offer

Once you find the right home, your offer is submitted. The seller can accept, reject or make a counter-offer.

Read more →
📋
Step 5: Survey & Valuation

A survey checks the property's condition, while the lender arranges a valuation to confirm it's worth the loan.

Read more →
⚖️
Step 6: Conveyancing (Legal Process)

Your solicitor handles the legal work, including searches, contracts and ensuring the property is legally yours.

Read more →
📝
Step 7: Exchange of Contracts

Both sides sign the legal documents and pay the deposit. The sale is now legally binding.

Read more →
🔑
Step 8: Completion Day

The remaining money is transferred and you receive the keys to your new home. Congratulations!

Read more →
💡 The full process usually takes 8–12 weeks on average, but it can be shorter or longer depending on your circumstances.

3. Costs You Should Know

When buying a home, there are several costs to consider beyond the purchase price.

🏦
Deposit
Typically 5% – 20%
🏷️
Stamp Duty
Varies by property price
⚖️
Legal Fees
£800 – £2,000+
🔍
Survey
£300 – £1,500
📦
Other Costs
Moving, insurance, etc.
