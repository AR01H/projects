Style 10

Step / Progress Carousel

Labelled step tabs · Single slide · Progress bar · Next & Back buttons

1

Save Your Deposit

Most lenders require between 5% and 20% of the property's value as a deposit. The larger your deposit, the lower your interest rate and monthly payments will be.

✓
Use a Help to Buy ISA or Lifetime ISA to boost your savings with a government bonus.
✓
Aim for at least 10% to access a wider range of mortgage deals.
✓
Use our Deposit Calculator to work out how long it will take to save.
2

Get a Mortgage in Principle

An Agreement in Principle (AIP) from a lender shows sellers you're serious and ready to proceed. It typically takes less than an hour and doesn't affect your credit score.

✓
Use a whole-of-market broker to find the best deal across all lenders.
✓
Check your credit report before applying — any errors can be corrected first.
✓
An AIP is usually valid for 60–90 days — renew it if needed.
3

Find Your Property

Start with a clear list of must-haves vs nice-to-haves, and focus on areas with good transport, schools and amenities for your lifestyle.

✓
Register with multiple estate agents and set up Rightmove / Zoopla alerts.
✓
Visit at different times of day to check noise, parking and neighbourhood feel.
✓
Research sold prices nearby to understand if a listing is fairly priced.
4

Make an Offer & Instruct a Solicitor

Once your offer is accepted, instruct a conveyancing solicitor immediately. Delays in instructing can slow the whole chain down.

✓
Get three conveyancing quotes — cheapest isn't always best.
✓
Commission a HomeBuyer Survey or Building Survey at this stage.
✓
Ask for a memorandum of sale to be issued promptly.
5

Exchange & Complete

Exchange of contracts makes the transaction legally binding. Completion is when you pay the balance and receive the keys — congratulations, the home is yours!

✓
Agree a completion date that works for all parties in the chain.
✓
Arrange buildings insurance from the day of exchange — not completion.
✓
Do a final walkthrough on completion day before handing over money.
